Polish Rogaliki

History of the Dish

Polish rogalik is made with yeast dough and sometimes filled with nuts, poppy seeds, or preserves. When Eastern Europeans immigrated to America, the pastry evolved into rugelach, shifting from yeast dough to quicker cream cheese–based doughs. By the mid 20th century, this American version became the standard, turning the pastry into more of a cookie than a traditional roll.

Description

Polish rogaliki are small, crescent-shaped pastries traditionally made from a soft yeast dough and filled with a sweet filling (jam). They are a classic homemade treat in Poland, often enjoyed with tea or coffee and popular for holidays.

Ingredients

Dough

  • 2 cups + 2 tsp flour
  • 1 teaspoon dried yeast
  • 1 1/8 stick cold butter
  • 5 tablespoons thick yogurt or sour cream
  • 2 egg yolks
  • Pinch of salt

Filling

  • 5 tablespoons jam

Directions

  1. Mix jam and flour in a bowl and set aside.
  2. Combine flour, yeast, and salt. Add butter, yolks, and yogurt.
  3. Form into a log, and cut into 3 equal parts.
  4. Roll out each part into a circle and cut into 8 triangles.
  5. Add jam to each triangle, roll into crescents, and curve the ends.
  6. Bake at 190°C / 375°F for 20–23 minutes until golden.
